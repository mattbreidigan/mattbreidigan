About

7 years. 30+ products.
Zero middlemen.

I started in chemical engineering, then taught myself to code and never looked back. That shift — from systems thinking in labs to systems thinking in software — still shapes how I approach every product I build.

I started building after watching a mission-critical system crash on launch day due to a broken handoff. Not a bug. A communication failure. The best software isn't just well-built. It's well-communicated.

Today I work directly with founders and product teams — no layers, no account managers. I ship products that last and never leave you guessing where things stand.

FAQ

Common questions

How does the process work?+
Discovery call first — understanding your goals, users, and constraints. Then a clear scope, timeline, and deliverables before a single line of code. No surprises, no scope creep, no guessing.
How long does a typical project take?+
Most projects run 4–12 weeks depending on complexity. A focused MVP can be live in under a month. A full product with custom design, backend, and integrations takes longer. You get a realistic timeline upfront — not an optimistic one.
What does working together actually look like?+
A direct line to me — not an account manager. Regular check-ins, open progress sharing, early flagging of issues. You'll always know where things stand without chasing me down.
Do you use AI in your development process?+
Yes, openly. Claude Code and Cursor are core to my workflow — faster builds, cleaner code, more time on the problems that actually matter. AI handles the grunt work. I handle the thinking and architecture.
How involved do I need to be?+
As involved as you want to be. I work well with hands-on founders and those who prefer to delegate and trust the process. What I need is clarity on the goal and timely feedback when I ask for it.
Will my product use AI too?+
Only if it genuinely improves the user experience — not to follow a trend. If AI can make your product smarter, faster, or more useful, I'll tell you exactly how and why before we build anything.
